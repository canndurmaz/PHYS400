2 Motivation — Elastic Constants as Design Criteria ~55 s · ~125 words
The selection of a structural alloy is principally governed by its elastic response, which for an isotropic continuum is summarised by two constants: Young's modulus E and Poisson's ratio ν. The table on the right enumerates representative engineering domains in which these quantities determine the design outcome — aerospace structures, turbine components, biomedical implants, automotive structures, civil engineering, and precision electromechanical devices. (pause) Direct experimental characterisation of these constants — via single-crystal tensile testing combined with ultrasonic measurement — remains costly in both time and sample preparation. Atomistic simulation offers a substantially less expensive alternative, provided that an accurate interatomic potential is available for the alloy system in question. This condition motivates the entire framework presented here.
Procedure: Reference the table by gesture; do not enumerate its rows.
3 Role of (E, ν) in Mechanical Design ~55 s · ~130 words
A brief enumeration clarifies why these two constants warrant the computational effort. Young's modulus enters the classical expressions for beam deflection, Euler buckling, natural frequency, and Hertzian contact pressure. Poisson's ratio determines the bulk modulus through the relation K = E divided by 3(1 − 2ν) — a quantity that diverges as ν approaches one-half — together with the plane-stress/plane-strain regime boundaries, stress concentration factors, and fracture-toughness corrections. (pause) For a cubic crystal the two engineering constants reduce analytically to two independent stiffness constants, C11 and C12, subject to the Born–Cauchy mechanical-stability criterion C11 > C12. This inequality will recur as the principal verification gate in Stage 5b.
Procedure: The Born–Cauchy inequality should be stated explicitly; it is the criterion invoked in the closing verification.
4 Atomistic Modelling — Interatomic Potentials and DFT ~65 s · ~150 words
An interatomic potential is a closed-form approximation of the many-body potential energy of a system of atoms; once specified, it yields forces by differentiation and permits classical molecular-dynamics simulation at scales inaccessible to first-principles methods. The historical development proceeds from pairwise potentials — inadequate for metallic bonding — through the Embedded Atom Method of Daw and Baskes, to the Modified EAM, which introduces angular partial densities and constitutes the established formalism for metallic systems of mixed bonding character. (pause) Density-functional theory provides the complementary first-principles route: a quantum-mechanical solver from which cohesive energies, lattice constants, bulk moduli, and elastic constants are obtained directly. DFT is of high accuracy but is limited to systems of order one thousand atoms; MEAM is empirical but accommodates six orders of magnitude more. The present work uses DFT as the reference against which the MEAM potential is calibrated.
Procedure: Underline the accuracy/scale trade-off explicitly — it is the rationale for the entire pipeline.
5 The MEAM Coverage Problem ~55 s · ~125 words
The principal obstacle addressed in this work is the fragmented coverage of the published MEAM literature. The table summarises four representative libraries; each is limited to a small element subset, and no single file spans the nine elements of the Al\,7075 specification. (pause) Direct merging is not admissible: the cross-interaction parameters between elements drawn from distinct libraries are undefined, and manual refitting does not scale — twelve elements give sixty-six binary pairs. The contemporary alternative — purely data-driven machine-learning potentials of the Behler–Parrinello, GAP, or MTP families — avoids the coverage problem but requires between ten thousand and one hundred thousand DFT configurations and forfeits the physical interpretability of MEAM. The present framework therefore retains the MEAM formalism and constructs the missing parameters automatically.
Procedure: Reference the table once by gesture; the “merged” row is the visual answer to the problem.
6 Proposed Methodology — Five-Stage Pipeline ~75 s · ~170 words
The proposed methodology comprises five sequential stages. Stage 1 generates random alloy compositions on the twelve-element simplex. Stage 2 evaluates each composition by LAMMPS molecular dynamics, yielding the cubic stiffness constants C11 and C12 for the entire corpus. Stage 3 performs Quantum-Espresso DFT calculations for each element and each binary pair, producing first-principles cohesive energies, lattice constants, bulk moduli, and formation energies. (pause) Stage 4 maps these reference values onto the MEAM parameter space, yielding an initialised twelve-element library that includes the previously undefined cross-terms. Stage 5 closes the loop: a neural-network surrogate of the MEAM-parameters-to-stiffness map is inverse-optimised, under L-BFGS, against the Stage-2 targets. The dashed branch denotes Stage 5a, an auxiliary surrogate that maps composition directly to the stiffness constants and is distributed as an interactive predictor independent of the MEAM-refitting loop.
Procedure: The longest segment of the talk. Indicate each stage with the laser pointer in turn; do not return to earlier stages.

Approximately seven thousand nine hundred configurations are generated; after the Stage-2 physicality filter, seven thousand eight hundred and fifteen are retained. 8 Stage 2 — Molecular-Dynamics Elastic Database ~60 s · ~135 words
Stage 2 evaluates each configuration in LAMMPS. A custom build of the code was required: the upstream distribution caps the number of MEAM species at five, whereas the merged library produced in Stage 4 contains twelve. The patched build raises the limit to twenty. (pause) For each configuration, a cubic supercell of approximately four nanometres is constructed from the lattice constant of the dominant element; atom types are then reassigned to the target mass fractions and the cell is minimised under anisotropic strain. A small axial strain of magnitude ten-to-the-minus-three is applied successively along each Cartesian direction, and the resulting stresses are central-differenced to recover C11 and C12. The scatter on the right shows that Mg- and Zn-rich compositions cluster near ν approaching one-half, which motivates training the surrogate on the stiffness constants rather than directly on (E, ν).
Procedure: The Mg/Zn cluster justifies the choice of training targets — state it explicitly.
9 Stage 3 — Quantum Espresso DFT References ~65 s · ~145 words
Stage 3 constitutes the first-principles reference layer. Quantum Espresso version 7.5 is driven through the Atomic Simulation Environment; a single process pool dispatches all self-consistent calculations, equation-of-state scans, and elastic-constant evaluations. The plane-wave cut-off is forty Rydberg, with Marzari–Vanderbilt smearing and a six-by-six-by-six Monkhorst–Pack mesh; spin polarisation is enabled automatically for iron, chromium, and manganese. (pause) For each element a seven-point equation-of-state scan is fitted with the Birch–Murnaghan equation, yielding the equilibrium lattice constant, the cohesive energy, and the bulk modulus. For each binary pair a single self-consistent calculation on the L12 or B2 reference structure yields the formation energy. The heat map summarises sixty-six binary pairs; signed values seed the MEAM cross-term energies directly, without further parameter fitting.
Procedure: The closing statement — seeding the cross-terms directly — is the formal hand-off from DFT to MEAM.

11 Stage 5a — Composition Surrogate ~55 s · ~125 words
Stage 5a is an auxiliary branch of the pipeline: a small neural network that consumes the LAMMPS elastic database directly and does not feed back into the MEAM refitting. It serves two functions — a consistency check on the molecular-dynamics output, and a fast interactive predictor of E and ν from an arbitrary composition vector. (pause) The architecture is a fully-connected network with layers of twelve, thirty-two, twenty, and two units, trained under Huber loss on C11 and C12, with a five-seed deep ensemble for uncertainty quantification. On the thirty-percent held-out split, the coefficient of determination for E is 0.77 and for ν is 0.52. On the Al\,7075 preset the surrogate returns 75.5 gigapascals and a Poisson's ratio of 0.334, consistent with the published reference range.
Procedure: Reference the deployed application image; do not propose a live demonstration.
12 Stage 5b — NN-Driven MEAM Parameter Optimisation ~65 s · ~150 words
Stage 5b constitutes the closed-loop core of the framework. A differentiable neural-network surrogate is trained to map the MEAM parameter vector onto the stiffness constants, with training data drawn from either Sobol or active-learning samples evaluated in LAMMPS. The L-BFGS optimiser minimises the Huber loss through the surrogate; the optimised parameters are subsequently re-evaluated in LAMMPS, new samples are appended, and the surrogate is retrained. Atomic checkpointing renders the procedure resumable. (pause) The surrogate Huber loss decays from approximately unity to a residual value of 0.16 over ten thousand training steps. Verification on thirty held-out alloys reveals the current limitations: of these thirty, only eleven satisfy the Born–Cauchy stability criterion; among those eleven, four lie within the twenty-percent engineering tolerance on both E and ν. The NN-optimised MEAM therefore does not yet constitute a deployable potential on the present twelve-element corpus.
Procedure: State the verification numbers without qualifying language; the closing slide addresses the interpretation.
13 Discussion and Outlook ~55 s · ~125 words
In summary, the contributions of the present work comprise the molecular-dynamics elastic database of seven thousand eight hundred and fifteen alloys, the DFT reference set covering ten elements, the merged twelve-element MEAM library, the deployed composition surrogate with a coefficient of determination of 0.77 on Young's modulus, and the resumable Stage-5b optimisation framework. (pause) The current limitations of Stage 5b are attributed to three causes: an under-determined inverse problem, a residual mismatch between the surrogate and the LAMMPS evaluator, and the absence of mechanical-stability constraints from the training loss. The planned extensions address these causes in turn: restricting optimisation to Sobol-sensitive cross-terms, expanding the active-learning corpus, incorporating physics-informed stability penalties, and introducing special-quasirandom-structure DFT references at intermediate compositions. The architecture is established; the remaining work is one of sampling and loss formulation.
