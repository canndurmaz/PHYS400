% --- Slide 2: Motivation ---
\begin{frame}{Motivation}
\begin{itemize}
  \item Elastic properties --- the cubic stiffness constants $C_{11}, C_{12}$, and the derived Young's modulus $E$ and Poisson's ratio $\nu$ --- are the principal design criteria for structural alloys.
  \item Direct experimental characterization is costly and slow; classical molecular dynamics can recover these properties at negligible cost, \emph{provided} an accurate interatomic potential is available.
  \item For metallic systems the \textbf{Modified Embedded Atom Method (MEAM)}\footnote{\tiny\textbf{MEAM}: Modified Embedded Atom Method.} remains the de-facto standard semi-empirical formalism.
\end{itemize}

\vspace{0.6em}
\begin{block}{Objective}
Construct MEAM potentials \emph{automatically} for arbitrary subsets of elements, with no manual parameter fitting and no experimental input.
\end{block}
\end{frame}

% --- Slide 3: Limitations of existing libraries ---
\begin{frame}{Limitations of Existing MEAM Libraries}
Published MEAM libraries cover disjoint subsets of elements; no single file spans the nine elements specified by the Al\,7075 alloy.

\vspace{0.5em}
\centering
\small
\begin{tabular}{l|cccccccccccc}
\toprule
\textbf{Library File} & Al & Co & Cr & Cu & Fe & Mg & Mn & Mo & Ni & Si & Ti & Zn \\
\midrule
AlMgZn           & \checkmark &   &   &   &   & \checkmark &   &   &   &   &   & \checkmark \\
AlSiCuMgFe       & \checkmark &   &   & \checkmark & \checkmark & \checkmark &   &   &   & \checkmark &   &   \\
CuMo             &   &   &   & \checkmark &   &   &   & \checkmark &   &   &   &   \\
FeMnNiTiCuCrCoAl & \checkmark & \checkmark & \checkmark & \checkmark & \checkmark &   & \checkmark &   & \checkmark &   & \checkmark &   \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}

\vspace{0.7em}
\raggedright
\begin{itemize}\small
  \item \textbf{Cross-interaction parameters} between elements drawn from distinct libraries are undefined; naive merging produces a potential that cannot be evaluated.
  \item \textbf{Purely data-driven interatomic potentials} (MLIPs)\footnote{\tiny\textbf{MLIP}: Machine-Learning Interatomic Potential.} avoid the coverage problem but require very large training corpora and forgo the physical interpretability of MEAM.
\end{itemize}
\end{frame}

\vspace{1em}
\begin{block}{Methodological Contribution}
A closed-loop framework in which a neural-network surrogate of the MEAM-to-stiffness map is inverse-optimized against MD-measured targets, enabling end-to-end MEAM construction from first-principles input alone --- no manual fitting, no experimental data.
\end{block}
