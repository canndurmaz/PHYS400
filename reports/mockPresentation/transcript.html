2 Motivation ~42 s · ~100 words
As a mechanical engineer graduate, the motivation is twofold. First, elastic properties — the cubic stiffness constants and the derived Young's modulus and Poisson's ratio — are the principal design criteria for structural alloys, yet direct experimental characterization remains costly and slow. (beat) Second, classical molecular dynamics can recover these properties at negligible cost — but only when supplied with an accurate interatomic potential. For metallic systems the Modified Embedded Atom Method remains the de-facto standard semi-empirical formalism. Our objective is to construct such potentials automatically, for arbitrary subsets of elements, with no manual parameter fitting.
Delivery: The (beat) separates "what's wrong with the status quo" from "what we propose." Let it land.
3 Limitations of Existing MEAM Libraries ~42 s · ~98 words
The principal obstacle is the fragmented coverage of existing MEAM libraries. Each published file covers only a small subset of elements; the table summarises four representative cases. The Al 7075 specification alone calls for nine elements, and no single library spans them all. (beat) Naively merging libraries fails: the cross-interaction parameters between elements drawn from distinct sources are undefined, so the combined potential cannot be evaluated. Purely data-driven machine-learning potentials avoid the coverage problem but require very large training corpora and forgo MEAM's physical interpretability. We therefore retain the MEAM formalism and supply the missing parameters automatically.
Delivery: Gesture to the table on "this table." Do not read its rows aloud — let the audience read it.
4 Methodology — Five-Stage Pipeline ~65 s · ~155 words
Our methodology comprises five sequential stages. Stage 1 generates random alloy compositions across the elements of interest. Stage 2 evaluates each composition with LAMMPS molecular dynamics to obtain the cubic stiffness tensor; Young's modulus and Poisson's ratio are derived analytically. Stage 3 performs density-functional calculations in Quantum Espresso for every element and every binary pair, producing first-principles cohesive energies, lattice constants, bulk moduli, and formation energies. (beat) Stage 4 maps those references onto the MEAM parameter space, yielding an initialized library that includes the previously undefined cross-terms. Stage 5 trains a neural-network surrogate of the parameters-to-stiffness map and inverse-optimizes the parameters against the Stage-2 targets. The dashed branch above is a separate composition-to-stiffness model — a linearity probe. It maps composition directly to E and ν, and exists to answer one question: can we represent E and ν as a linear function of composition? If we could, MEAM would be unnecessary — a linear regression would suffice. The observed non-linearity is what justifies a physics-based potential.
Delivery: Longest slide. Sweep your pointer left-to-right across the stage boxes as you name each one. Slow on Stage 5 (closed-loop core) and on the linearity-probe explanation — that motivates why MEAM is needed at all.
