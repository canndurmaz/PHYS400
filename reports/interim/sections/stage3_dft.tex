\subsection{Stage 3 --- DFT Reference Calculations}
\label{sec:stage3}

The DFT reference stage uses Quantum Espresso \citep{giannozzi2009}
(version~7.5) driven through the Atomic Simulation Environment
\citep{ase2017}. Before any production calculations were carried out, the
QE--ASE coupling was validated on bulk FCC aluminium with a three-point
sanity check (single-point SCF energy in the expected $-540$ to
$-530$\,eV window, residual atomic forces below $0.01$\,eV/\AA, and
diagonal-only stress tensor), all of which pass at the chosen plane-wave
cut-offs. For each requested element the following quantities are then
computed:

\begin{enumerate}
	\item the equilibrium lattice constant $a_0$, cohesive energy
	      $E_\mathrm{coh}$ and bulk modulus $B$ from a seven-point
	      equation-of-state scan fit to a Birch--Murnaghan equation
	      \citep{birch};
	\item an isolated-atom reference energy in a large empty box, used
	      to convert the bulk total energy into a true cohesive energy;
	\item the cubic elastic constants $C_{11}$ and $C_{12}$ from a
	      stress--strain calculation analogous to the LAMMPS protocol
	      of Section~\ref{sec:stage2}.
\end{enumerate}
For each unordered binary pair $(i,j)$ a single SCF calculation is run on
the L1$_2$ or B2 reference structure (chosen based on the lattice types of
the two elements) to obtain the formation energy
$E_\mathrm{form}(i,j)$.

The plane-wave cut-off is set to $E_\mathrm{cut}=40$\,Ry with a charge
density cut-off of 320\,Ry, Marzari--Vanderbilt smearing of width
0.02\,Ry, and a $6\!\times\!6\!\times\!6$ Monkhorst--Pack mesh.
Spin-polarised calculations are activated automatically for the magnetic
elements Fe, Cr and Mn. Pseudopotentials are downloaded from the official
Quantum Espresso pseudopotential library on demand. The EOS strain points
and the binary-pair SCF calculations are dispatched to a process pool,
which made it possible to obtain the full reference set on a single
workstation.

The current DFT reference dataset covers ten elements (Al, Cu, Si, Ti, Zn,
Cr, Fe, Mg, Mn and Mo) and 40 binary pairs. The fitted
elemental properties for a representative subset are listed in
Table~\ref{tab:dft_elements}. The Mn entry was retained at its tabulated
value because the EOS scan failed to converge for the simplified
$\alpha$-Mn approximation, and the corresponding cross-terms therefore
inherit a higher uncertainty. The fitted EOS curves are shown in
Figure~\ref{fig:eos}.

The 40 binary formation energies $E_\mathrm{form}(i,j)$ obtained from
the L1$_2$/B2 SCF references are summarised as a symmetric heatmap in
Figure~\ref{fig:formation}. Negative values (blue) indicate exothermic
mixing, expected for compatible $sd$/$sp$ pairs such as Al--Ti or
Cu--Ti, while strongly positive values (red) flag elements with low
mutual solubility (notably the Mg/Zn pair against most transition
metals). These signed values feed Eq.~(2) of Stage~4 directly and
therefore propagate, unmodified, to the cross-term $E_c(i,j)$ entries
of the merged MEAM library.
