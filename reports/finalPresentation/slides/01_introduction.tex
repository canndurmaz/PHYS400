\begin{frame}{Motivation: Why Elastic Constants Drive Alloy Design}
\begin{itemize}
  \item Structural alloys (Al\,7075, Inconel, Ti\,6Al--4V, $\dots$) are
        selected on the basis of their \textbf{stiffness}, \textbf{ductility} and
        \textbf{Poisson response}, all of which derive from a small set of
        elastic constants.
  \item Direct experimental measurement is costly: a single
        single-crystal tensile + ultrasonic characterization
        requires weeks of sample preparation.
  \item Classical \textbf{molecular dynamics} (MD) can compute the full
        elastic tensor in minutes per composition, but \emph{only if
        an accurate interatomic potential is available}.
  \item For metallic systems the \textbf{Modified Embedded Atom
        Method} (MEAM)~\citep{baskes1992,lee2001}\footnote{\tiny MEAM = Modified Embedded Atom
        Method --- a semi-empirical formalism that adds angular
        dependence to the EAM embedding function.} remains the
        de-facto formalism.
\end{itemize}

\vspace{0.4em}
\begin{block}{Objective}
Build MEAM potentials \emph{automatically} for arbitrary subsets of
elements, with no manual parameter fitting and no experimental input ---
a closed loop from first principles to molecular dynamics.
\end{block}
\end{frame}
