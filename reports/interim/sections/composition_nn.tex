\subsection{Auxiliary Composition Surrogate (MD sanity-check branch)}
\label{sec:comp_nn}

This subsection describes the divergent ML branch shown as the dashed
side-arrow in Figure~\ref{fig:pipeline}. Unlike Stages~3--4, which seed
the main pipeline that will eventually be closed by neural-network
parameter optimization, the present branch consumes the Stage~2 MD
output directly and never feeds back into the potential-fitting flow.
Its sole purpose is to act as a fast sanity check on the consistency
of the LAMMPS-generated dataset --- if a composition-only network
cannot recover even an approximate trend in $(E,\nu)$ from the
dataset, the MD output itself is suspect --- and to provide a
ready-to-use predictor for novel compositions.

Each input vector has 10 entries (one mass fraction per element in
$\{$Al, Co, Cr, Cu, Fe, Mg, Mn, Ni, Ti, Zn$\}$); the network has the
architecture $10\to32\to20\to2$ with ReLU activations and is trained
under a 70/30 train/validation split with the Adam optimizer.

\paragraph{Targets and Huber loss.}
The regression targets are the LAMMPS-evaluated $(E,\nu)$ pairs
produced by the Stage~2 strain protocol described in
Section~\ref{sec:stage2}. Records with $\nu\!\geq\!0.48$ are dropped to
remove configurations that lie close to the mechanical-stability bound
and that destabilise the regression in composition space. The training
loss is \textbf{Huber} ($\delta=1$ in scaled units) rather than MSE,
which bounds the gradient contribution of outlier samples (Mg/Zn-rich
compositions whose LAMMPS runs occasionally yield extreme moduli).

\paragraph{Stopping gate.}
Training stops as soon as the \emph{mean} relative error on the
derived $(E,\nu)$ over the held-out 30\,\% validation set falls within
$\mathtt{MAX\_ERROR\_PCT}=20\,\%$ for both quantities, i.e.\
$\overline{|\Delta E|/E}\le 20\,\%$ \emph{and}
$\overline{|\Delta \nu|/\nu}\le 20\,\%$. This replaces an earlier
per-sample gate that required \emph{every} validation alloy to be
within 20\,\%; that criterion was dominated by a small number of
Mg/Zn-rich outliers and tended to drive the optimiser into false
restarts just to satisfy the worst sample. The mean-based gate
targets the bulk behaviour of the surrogate while still leaving the
individual residuals visible in
Figures~\ref{fig:aux_parity_val}--\ref{fig:aux_err}; per-sample
pass/fail counts at the same 20\,\% threshold are still emitted as
diagnostics.
